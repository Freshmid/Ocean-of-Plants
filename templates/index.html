About Soil

Soil is the second most important factor in growing healthy vegetable plants. There are many types of soil. We pick 4 types of soil to identify how good your soil is for gardening or farming.

Clay Soil

Clay is a soft, loose, earthy material containing particles with a grain size of less than 4 micrometres. Clay Soil is a heavy soil type that benefits from high nutrients but remain wet and cold in winter and dry out in summer.

Silt Soil

Silt is granular material of a size between sand and clay and composed mostly of broken grains of quartz. As silt soils compromise of medium sized particles they are well drained and hold moisture well. Silt soil can be easily compacted and are prone to washing away with rain.

Sandy Soil

Sandy Soil is light, warm, dry and tends to be acidic and low in nutrients. They are quicker to warm up in spring than clay soils but tend to dry out in summer and suffer from low nutrients that are washed away by rain.

Loam Soil

Loam soil is a mixture of sand, silt and clay that are combined to avoid the negative effects of each type. These soils are fertile, easy to work with and provide good drainage. As the soils are a perfect balance of soil particles, they are considered to be a gardeners best friend, but still benefit from topping up with additional organic matter.
